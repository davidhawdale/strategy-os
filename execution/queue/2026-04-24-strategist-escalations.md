# Strategist Escalations to Governor

**From:** strategist
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Mode:** BUILD (initial register construction)
**Re:** DC Thomson Scottish Borders extension hypothesis register

Two load-bearing assumptions in the register depend on data only DC Thomson can supply. The strategy outcome materially depends on the answers. Both are flagged in the register as ESCALATED.

---

## Escalation 1: Marginal cost of platform extension

**What is at stake:** The unit economics in Section 3 assume DCT's existing Pugpig Bolt platform, payments stack, identity system, and editorial CMS extend to a third regional brand at marginal cost (no rebuild). If this is wrong -- if a third brand requires £200k+ engineering build or significant ongoing platform cost -- the cost structure shifts materially and the BOOTSTRAP-equivalent thresholds may not be met even in the base scenario.

**Question for governor:** What is DCT's internal CTO / engineering view on the marginal cost of standing up *The Borders Journal* (or equivalent) on the existing platform stack? Specifically:
- Is the Pugpig Bolt instance multi-tenant, or does each brand need its own deployment?
- What is the marginal monthly platform cost per additional brand?
- What is the one-off engineering effort to add a new brand (in person-weeks or £)?

**If the answer is "marginal" (small £):** Register holds; proceed to Gap Definer destruction.
**If the answer is "material" (£200k+):** Section 3 needs revision -- adds a one-off cost that pushes payback further out and may require Phase 1 budget reallocation.

---

## Escalation 2: Central overhead allocation policy

**What is at stake:** The unit economics assume central overhead (HR, finance, legal, brand) is allocated to the new title at c.15-20% of direct opex (i.e. marginal allocation, not standalone allocation). This is the standard treatment for an extension of an existing operation but it is a policy choice. If DCT internal accounting allocates standalone overhead to the new title (treating it as a fully-loaded P&L), the cost base roughly doubles at the overhead line and the unit becomes much harder to defend.

**Question for governor:** What overhead allocation policy will the Borders extension be judged against?
- Marginal (treats it as an extension, allocates only incremental overhead)?
- Standalone (treats it as a full P&L, allocates full per-title overhead)?
- Or some hybrid (e.g. 3-year ramp from marginal to standalone)?

**If marginal:** Register holds.
**If standalone:** Section 3 cost structure needs revision -- Phase 2 traction targets may need to lift to 4,500-6,000 paying subs to support standalone overhead.

## Note on what is NOT being escalated

I am NOT escalating the strategic question of "should we proceed". That is a values + ground-truth decision, not a strategist call. The register is constructed to make the case visible:

- The opportunity exists (problem real, segment defined, unit economics achievable in base/optimistic)
- The opportunity is tight (pessimistic scenario kills it; margin of safety thin)
- Validation gaps remain (resident interviews, channel pilots, internal cost confirmation)

The right next step is Gap Definer destruction, then governor decision on whether to fund a Phase 0 validation exercise (resident interviews + landing-page test + carrier tender for print) before committing to Phase 1.
