## Section 00: Mode Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "We will raise funding" | Can we actually raise? At what terms? |
| "We can bootstrap to profitability" | Is cash flow timeline realistic? |
| "Market timing is right for scale" | Why now? What if delayed? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Funding availability at expected valuation
- Time to profitability matches runway
- Market window remains open

---

## Section 03: Opportunity Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "TAM is $X billion" | Source? Growing or shrinking? |
| "We can capture X% of market" | What's the basis for share estimate? |
| "Market is growing at X%" | Will growth continue? |
| "Timing is right because..." | What if timing assumption wrong? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Market size estimates are accurate
- Growth rates continue
- No market disruption coming
- Regulatory environment stable

---

## Section 04: Segment Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "[Segment] exists and is identifiable" | Can we actually find them? |
| "[Segment] is large enough" | How many? Growing? |
| "[Segment] behaves as described" | Based on what evidence? |
| "We can reach [segment] via [channel]" | Proven or assumed? |
| "[Segment] has budget for this" | Who controls budget? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Segment is homogeneous enough
- Segment is reachable at reasonable CAC
- Segment has decision-making authority
- Segment behavior is predictable

**Red flags:**
- Segment defined by demographics only (not behavior)
- "Everyone" or "all businesses" as segment
- No observable filtering criteria

## Section 05: Problem Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Problem is severe" | How do we know? Severity score basis? |
| "Problem occurs frequently" | How often? Evidence? |
| "Problem costs $X" | Calculated or estimated? |
| "Current solutions inadequate" | Why? What's been tried? |
| "People want this solved" | Active search or latent need? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Problem is top priority for buyer
- Problem is recognized as problem
- Problem is solvable at acceptable cost
- People are actively seeking solutions

**Red flags:**
- "Nice to have" not "must have"
- No quantified cost of problem
- No evidence of active search behavior

## Section 06: Competitive Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Competitors don't do X" | Will they? Can they? |
| "We're differentiated by X" | Does customer care about X? |
| "Switching cost is low" | Have customers actually switched? |
| "Incumbents are slow" | What if they respond? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Competitors won't copy our approach
- Our differentiation matters to buyers
- Switching cost/effort is acceptable
- Incumbents won't acquire or crush us

**Red flags:**
- Differentiation is technical, not customer-valued
- "No competitors" (almost never true)
- Underestimating incumbent response

---

## Section 07: UVP Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "[Benefit] resonates with [segment]" | Tested or assumed? |
| "Customers will understand our value" | Immediately or after education? |
| "[Benefit] is better than alternatives" | By how much? Does it matter? |
| "Emotional/functional appeal works" | Which drives purchase? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Messaging communicates value clearly
- Benefit is valued over competitor benefits
- Value can be demonstrated quickly
- Promise can be delivered

**Red flags:**
- Value prop is feature list
- No "so what" / outcome specified
- Requires customer education to understand

---

## Section 08: Unfair Advantage Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Network effects will kick in" | At what scale? How long? |
| "Switching costs protect us" | From what exactly? |
| "Data advantage compounds" | How much data needed? |
| "Brand will become moat" | How long to build? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Moat can be built before competition arrives
- Moat is actually defensible
- Moat matters to customers (not just investors)
- Time to moat matches runway

**Red flags:**
- Moat is "first mover advantage" (rarely real)
- Moat requires scale we don't have
- Moat is technology (usually copyable)

---

## Section 09: Solution Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Features solve the problem" | Which features? Evidence? |
| "Users will adopt [behavior]" | Change from current behavior? |
| "MVP is sufficient to demonstrate value" | What's cut? Does it still work? |
| "Growth model will work" | PLG/Network/Traditional fit? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Feature set maps to stated problems
- Users will change behavior for our solution
- MVP delivers enough value to convert
- Growth model fits product and market

**Red flags:**
- Feature list without problem mapping
- Growth model doesn't match ACV/buyer
- MVP missing critical value component

## Section 12: Revenue Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "[Segment] will pay $X" | Tested or guessed? |
| "Free tier converts at X%" | Benchmark or hope? |
| "Tier structure captures market" | Coverage or gaps? |
| "Pricing matches value delivered" | Value quantified? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Willingness to pay at assumed price point
- Conversion rate from free to paid
- Expansion revenue from existing customers
- Annual contracts vs monthly

**Red flags:**
- Pricing based on cost-plus not value
- No testing of price sensitivity
- Single tier (no segmentation)

---

## Section 13: Metrics Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "CAC will be $X" | Based on what channels? |
| "LTV will be $X" | Churn assumption? |
| "Conversion rate of X%" | Funnel benchmark? |
| "LTV:CAC of X:1" | Achievable or aspirational? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- CAC at assumed channel mix
- Retention at assumed rate
- Expansion revenue compounds
- Payback period acceptable

**Red flags:**
- Unit economics require scale to work
- LTV:CAC < 3:1
- Payback > 18 months

---

## Section 14: Cost Assumptions

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Cost structure is X" | Fixed vs variable mix? |
| "Gross margin of X%" | Includes what? |
| "Can scale efficiently" | What breaks at scale? |

**Common hidden assumptions:**
- Cost structure remains stable
- Gross margin sustainable
- No hidden costs at scale

## Risk Assessment Matrix

### Impact Guidelines

| Score | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| 5 | Business fails | Wrong market, no demand |
| 4 | Major pivot | Wrong segment, wrong pricing model |
| 3 | Significant rework | Wrong channels, wrong features |
| 2 | Minor adjustment | Pricing tweaks, messaging changes |
| 1 | Negligible | Cosmetic, easily fixed |

### Uncertainty Guidelines

| Score | Description | Evidence Level |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 5 | Pure guess | No data, no research |
| 4 | Weak signal | Secondary research only |
| 3 | Some evidence | Interviews, surveys |
| 2 | Good evidence | Pilots, early customers |
| 1 | Validated | Statistical significance |

## Extraction Checklist

For each canvas section:

1. [ ] Read section completely
2. [ ] Identify statements presented as facts
3. [ ] Question: "What must be true for this to work?"
4. [ ] Extract each implicit assumption
5. [ ] Categorize (market/customer/problem/value/pricing/channel)
6. [ ] Score impact (1-5)
7. [ ] Score uncertainty (1-5)
8. [ ] Assign priority (P0/P1/P2/P3)
9. [ ] Write "if wrong" consequence
10. [ ] Define evidence needed
